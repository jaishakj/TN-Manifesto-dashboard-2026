EDITORIAL VERDICT · THREE PARTIES
DMK governs on paper. TVK disrupts where it chooses. ADMK lists without a plan.
DMK (423/500) leads every single category. Its manifesto is institutionally grounded — fiscal data, monitoring commissions, timelines, sectoral ecosystems. Nowhere does it say "steps will be taken" without specifying which steps, by whom, and by when. The $1 trillion economy plan, AI Mission 2.0, Tamil LLM, and semiconductor policy read like a governing document, not a wishlist.

TVK (281/500) ranks second — a genuinely impressive debut for a two-year-old party. Its core promises on youth employment (₹4,000 unemployment allowance, ₹20 lakh interest-free loans, 5 lakh internships, 75% local jobs with GST incentives) are specific and credible. The "Creative Entrepreneurs Scheme" and anti-drug protection zones are policy-grade ideas. TVK's weakness is scope — large swaths of the scorecard (fisheries, weavers, elderly, SL Tamils, overseas Tamils, financial management) are barely addressed. It is a manifesto for one demographic fighting on one front.

ADMK (255/500) ranks third. Its document is a list of 297 welfare promises with no fiscal roadmap, no institutional mechanisms beyond "a committee will be formed," and no forward vision for technology, climate, or governance. Its best scores (5–6) come from direct transfer schemes that require no institutional design. Its worst (3) come from every category requiring strategic thinking.

Final ranking: DMK 423 · TVK 281 · ADMK 255 · out of 500 total points.
